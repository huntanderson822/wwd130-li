Freddie Mercury (Queen)

Born: 1946 in Zanzibar
Died: 1991

Known for incredible vocal range, flamboyant stage presence, and writing iconic songs like "Bohemian Rhapsody". Mercury helped define arena rock and pushed the boundaries of theatrical rock performance.

Mick Jagger (The Rolling Stones)

Born: 1943 in England

Jagger is known for his gritty vocals, electric stage energy, and fronting one of the most influential rock bands. He helped shape the rebellious spirit of rock with hits like "Satisfaction" and "Start Me Up."

Elvis Presley (The King of Rock 'n' Roll)

Born: 1935 in Mississippi
Died: 1977

Presley revolutionized popular music by blending country, gospel, and blues. His charisma and hits like "Hound Dog" made him the global icon of early rock 'n' roll.

Kurt Cobain (Nirvana)

Born: 1967 in Washington
Died: 1994

Cobain led the grunge movement with emotional, raw songwriting. His music became the voice of Generation X, with anthems like "Smells Like Teen Spirit."

Jim Morrison (The Doors)

Born: 1943 in Florida
Died: 1971

Known for poetic lyrics and a mysterious stage persona, Morrison helped define psychedelic rock with songs like "Riders on the Storm" and "The End."
